Worked Example: Corrosion Rate and Degradation Analysis

Calculate the corrosion rate of a metallic alloy and analyze the degradation of a polymer in an aggressive environment.

Objectives:

  1. Determine the corrosion rate of a metal using mass loss data.
  2. Analyze the chemical degradation of a polymer in a solvent.

Key Concept: Corrosion and Degradation Mechanisms

Corrosion is the deterioration of a metal due to electrochemical reactions with its environment. The corrosion rate (\( R \)) can be calculated using mass loss data:

\[ R = \frac{K \Delta m}{A t \rho} \]

Where \( K \) is a constant (e.g., \( 8.76 \times 10^4 \) for \( \text{mm/year} \)), \( \Delta m \) is the mass loss, \( A \) is the surface area, \( t \) is time, and \( \rho \) is the density.

Polymer degradation often involves chemical reactions with solvents, leading to changes in molecular weight and mechanical properties.

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine the corrosion rate and degradation effects for:

  • An Iron sample exposed to an acidic environment for \( 30 \, \text{days} \). The initial mass is \( 200 \, \text{g} \), and the mass loss is \( 5 \, \text{g} \). The surface area is \( 0.02 \, \text{m}^2 \), and the density of Iron is \( 7.87 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \).
  • A Polycarbonate sample immersed in Acetone for \( 10 \, \text{days} \). The sample's mass decreases by \( 2 \, \text{\%} \) due to solvent attack, affecting its tensile strength. The initial tensile strength is \( 70 \, \text{MPa} \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the Corrosion Rate for Iron

Convert the mass loss to \( \text{g/cm}^3 \):

\[ \Delta m = 5 \, \text{g}, \quad \rho = 7.87 \, \text{g/cm}^3 \]

Calculate the corrosion rate using the formula:

\[ R = \frac{8.76 \times 10^4 \times 5}{0.02 \times 30 \times 7.87} = 93.1 \, \text{mm/year} \]

Step 2: Analyze Degradation of Polycarbonate

The mass loss of \( 2 \, \text{\%} \) indicates chemical degradation. Assume a linear relationship between mass loss and tensile strength reduction:

\[ \text{New Tensile Strength} = 70 \, \text{MPa} \times (1 - 0.02) = 68.6 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 3: Interpretation of Results

The corrosion rate of Iron is relatively high, indicating significant material loss in the acidic environment. The reduction in tensile strength of Polycarbonate suggests a loss in mechanical integrity due to solvent exposure.

Discuss methods to mitigate corrosion and polymer degradation.

Consider protective coatings, material selection, and environmental control as strategies to minimize deterioration.
