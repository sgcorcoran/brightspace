Summary of Equations: Failure Mechanisms

Fracture Mechanics

  • Stress Intensity Factor (K): \[ K = Y \sigma \sqrt{\pi a} \]
    • \( K \): Stress intensity factor (MPa√m)
    • \( Y \): Dimensionless geometry factor
    • \( \sigma \): Applied stress (MPa)
    • \( a \): Crack length (m)
  • Critical Crack Size (\(a_{crit}\)): \[ a_{crit} = \frac{1}{\pi}\left(\frac{K_{Ic}}{Y\sigma}\right)^2 \]
    • \( K_{Ic} \): Fracture toughness (MPa√m)
  • Critical Stress (\(\sigma_{crit}\)): \[ \sigma_{crit} = \frac{K_{Ic}}{Y\sqrt{\pi a}} \]
  • Griffith Criterion (Brittle Fracture): \[ \sigma_c = \sqrt{\frac{2E\gamma_s}{\pi a}} \]
    • \( \sigma_c \): Critical stress for fracture (MPa)
    • \( E \): Young’s modulus (MPa)
    • \( \gamma_s \): Specific surface energy (J/m²)

Fatigue (Crack Growth)

  • Fully Integrated Form of the Paris Equation (\(m \ne 2\)): \[ N_f = \frac{2}{C(m-2)(\Delta \sigma)^m \pi^{m/2}Y^m}\left[a_i^{1-\frac{m}{2}} - a_f^{1-\frac{m}{2}}\right] \]
    • \( N_f \): Number of cycles to failure
    • \( C, m \): Material-specific Paris constants
    • \( \Delta \sigma \): Stress range (MPa)
    • \( Y \): Geometry factor (dimensionless)
    • \( a_i \): Initial crack length (m)
    • \( a_f \): Final (critical) crack length (m)

Creep

  • Larson-Miller Parameter (Predicting creep lifetime): \[ LMP = T(\log t_r + C) \]
    • \(LMP\): Larson-Miller parameter
    • \(t_r\): Time to rupture (hours)
    • \(C\): Material constant (typically ~20)

Example Calculation using Polynomial Fit:

If a polynomial fit to data gives \(LMP = 15000 + 250\log \sigma - 10(\log \sigma)^2\), for \(\sigma = 100\) MPa:

  • \[LMP = 15000 + 250\log(100) - 10[\log(100)]^2\] \[LMP = 15000 + 500 - 40 = 15460\]
