Temperature Dependence of the Diffusivity

Objectives:

  1. Use the Arrhenius equation to calculate the diffusivity at a given temperature.

Given Problem Statement

Calculate the diffusivity of Ni in Cu at 500 °C

  • Nickel in Copper: \( T = 500\, °C\), with \( D_0 = 3.8 \times 10^{-5}\,\frac{m^2}{sec} \) and \( Q = 228\, \text{kJ/mol} \).
  • \(R=8.314 \,J\, mol^{-1}\, K^{-1}\)

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Nickel

The Arrhenius equation is:

\[ D = D_0 \exp\left(-\frac{Q}{RT}\right) \]

Substituting given values:

\[ D = \left(3.8 \times 10^{-5}\,\frac{m^2}{sec}\right) \exp\left(-\frac{228 \times 10^3\, \frac{J}{mol}}{8.314\, \frac{J}{mol\,K}\times (500+273)\,K}\right) \approx 1.5 \times 10^{-20}\, m^2/sec\]
