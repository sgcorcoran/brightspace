Worked Example: Fatigue Life in Polystyrene

Compare fatigue life for virgin and UV-damaged material polystyrene.

Objectives:

  1. Apply the integrated form of Paris’ Law to calculate cycles to failure.
  2. Compare fatigue behavior of virgin vs. UV-degraded polystyrene under the same mechanical loading.

Problem Statement

A 6-inch (0.152 m) long cantilevered polystyrene ruler is deflected by applying a 1 N tip force and then released (minimum stress equals zero). A surface crack of length \( a_0 = 0.1 \, \text{mm} \) is present at the root. Determine the number of cycles to failure \( N \) using Paris’ Law for:

  • Virgin polystyrene (\( C = 1 \times 10^{-9} \), \( m = 3.5 \), \( K_{IC} = 1.0 \, \text{MPa}\sqrt{m} \))
  • UV-degraded polystyrene (\( C = 5 \times 10^{-7} \), \( m = 5.5 \), \( K_{IC} = 0.4 \, \text{MPa}\sqrt{m} \))

Setup

General Fatigue Life Equation

The integrated form of Paris’ Law is:

\[ N = \frac{2}{(2 - m) \cdot C \cdot (\Delta \sigma)^m \cdot \pi^{m/2}} \left( a_c^{1 - m/2} - a_0^{1 - m/2} \right) \]

Bending Stress

The bending stress at the root of a cantilevered beam (such as a ruler loaded at the end) can be calculated using:

\[ \sigma = \frac{M c}{I} = \frac{F L \cdot h/2}{\frac{b h^3}{12}} = \frac{6 F L}{b h^2} \]

Where:
– \( F \): applied tip force (N)
– \( L \): length of the ruler (m)
– \( h \): thickness (m)
– \( b \): width (m)
– \( I \): area moment of inertia
– \( c = h/2 \): distance from the neutral axis to the outer fiber

This stress is used as the nominal stress in Paris' Law if the crack is located at the root where the maximum bending stress occurs.

Common Geometry and Stress

  • Length \( L = 0.152 \, \text{m} \), Force \( F = 1 \, \text{N} \)
  • Width \( b = 0.025 \, \text{m} \), Thickness \( h = 0.003 \, \text{m} \)
  • Moment of inertia: \( I = \frac{b h^3}{12} = 5.625 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{m}^4 \)
  • Bending stress: \( \sigma = \frac{M c}{I} = \frac{F L \cdot h/2}{I} = 40.5 \, \text{MPa} \)
  • \(\Delta \sigma = \sigma_{max}-\sigma_{min} = 40.5 - 0 = 40.5\, \text{MPa}\)

Calculations

Virgin Polystyrene

  • \( C = 1 \times 10^{-9} \), \( m = 3.5 \), \( K_{IC} = 1.0 \)
  • \( a_c = \left( \frac{1.0}{40.5 \cdot \sqrt{\pi}} \right)^2 = 1.54 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m} \)
  • Exponent: \( 1 - \frac{m}{2} = -0.75 \)
  • Substitute into integrated Paris Law:

\[ N = \frac{2}{(2 - 3.5)(1 \times 10^{-9})(40.5)^{3.5} \pi^{1.75}} \left( (1.54 \times 10^{-4})^{-0.75} - (1 \times 10^{-4})^{-0.75} \right) \]

\( \Rightarrow N \approx 3.3 \times 10^5 \, \text{cycles} \)

UV-Damaged Polystyrene

  • \( C = 5 \times 10^{-7} \), \( m = 5.5 \), \( K_{IC} = 0.4 \)
  • \( a_c = \left( \frac{0.4}{40.5 \cdot \sqrt{\pi}} \right)^2 = 9.85 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{m} \)
  • Exponent: \( 1 - \frac{m}{2} = -1.75 \)
  • Substitute into integrated Paris Law:

\[ N = \frac{2}{(2 - 5.5)(5 \times 10^{-7})(40.5)^{5.5} \pi^{2.75}} \left( (9.85 \times 10^{-6})^{-1.75} - (1 \times 10^{-4})^{-1.75} \right) \]

\( \Rightarrow N \approx 81 \, \text{cycles} \)

Conclusion:

UV degradation causes a dramatic drop in fatigue life—from hundreds of thousands of cycles to fewer than 100.
