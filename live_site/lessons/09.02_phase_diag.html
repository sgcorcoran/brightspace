Phase Diagrams Part 2: Inverse Lever Rule

Interpreting Binary Phase Diagrams

Understanding a binary phase diagram involves identifying the phases present, their compositions, and the relative amounts (weight fractions) at a given temperature and composition.

Step-by-Step Example (Cu-Ni Alloy)

Consider a Cu-Ni alloy with 35 wt% Ni at different temperatures:

The region between the liquidus and solidus lines contains two phases: solid \(\alpha\) and liquid, \(L\). Above the liquidus line is liquid only and below the solidus line is solid only.

Phases Present at 1300, 1250, and 1190°C

  • 1300°C (point a): One phase - liquid solution (L).
  • 1250°C (point c): Two phases - solid solution (α) and liquid solution (L).
  • 1190°C (point e): One phase - solid solution (α).

Estimating Phase Compositions: Tie-line

A tie-line (straight line) is drawn only in two phase regions. The second figure above shows the tie-line at 1260, 1250, and 1220°C. The tie-line extends from one side of the two phase region to the other. In this case, liquid is on the left side of the tie-line and \(\alpha\) is on the right. The two end points give the composition of each phase respectively.

Consider the tie-line at 1250°C, this tie line intersects the liquidus line on the left at 32 wt% Ni which is therefore the composition of the liquid phase. The intersection on the right side (with solidus line) gives the \(\alpha\) composition which is 43wt% Ni. To summarize:

The tie-line at 1250°C gives:

  • \(C_{L}=\text{32 wt% Ni + 68 wt% Cu}\)
  • \(C_{\alpha}=\text{43 wt% Ni + 57 wt% Cu}\)

Weight (mass) fractions of phases: Inverse Lever Rule

Apply the inverse lever rule to the tie line. The tie line drawn in the previous problem on the phase diagram is reproduced below. Notice that the left and right sides of the tie line are now labeled as liquid, \(L\) and solid, \(\alpha\) and the compositions are identified as found previously, 32 and 43 wt% Ni respectively. Also indicated is the composition of the overall alloy, \(C_0=\text{35 wt% Ni}\).

The relative lengths of the two sides of the tie line are also indicated on the figure above and calculated below. The inverse lever rule says that the fraction of a phase is given by the relative length opposite the side of the phase as shown in the figure below. For this figure, \(\alpha\) is indicated on the right side, therefore the fractional amount of \(\alpha\) is given by the left side, i.e. 0.27. The fractional amount of liquid is likewise given by 0.73.

  • Weight fraction calculations: \[ W_L = \frac{43-35}{43-32} = 0.73 \]
    \[ W_α = \frac{35-32}{43-32} = 0.27 \]
