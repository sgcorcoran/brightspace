Worked Example: Thermal Expansion Calculation

Determine the change in length of a metallic and a polymeric material due to thermal expansion.

Objectives:

  1. Apply the linear thermal expansion equation to calculate dimensional changes.
  2. Compare thermal expansion behavior between metals and polymers.

Key Concept: Thermal Expansion

Thermal expansion describes the change in dimensions of a material with temperature. The linear expansion equation is:

\[ \Delta L = \alpha L_0 \Delta T \]

Where \( \alpha \) is the coefficient of linear expansion, \( L_0 \) is the original length, and \( \Delta T \) is the change in temperature.

Expanded Problem Statement

Calculate the change in length for:

  • An aluminum rod (\( \alpha = 23 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1} \)), initially \( 1 \, \text{m} \) long, with a temperature increase of \( 100 \, \text{K} \).
  • A PVC rod (\( \alpha = 70 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1} \)), initially \( 0.5 \, \text{m} \) long, with a temperature increase of \( 50 \, \text{K} \).

Solution

Metallic Material: Aluminum

Calculate the change in length:

\[ \Delta L = \alpha L_0 \Delta T = (23 \times 10^{-6})(1)(100) = 2.3 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m} = 2.3 \, \text{mm} \]

Polymeric Material: PVC

Calculate the change in length:

\[ \Delta L = (70 \times 10^{-6})(0.5)(50) = 1.75 \times 10^{-3} \, \text{m} = 1.75 \, \text{mm} \]
