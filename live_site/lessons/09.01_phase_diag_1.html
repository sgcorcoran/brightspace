Introduction to Phase Diagrams

Phase diagrams are visual representations of the phases present and their compositions under various temperature and overall alloy composition. Understanding these diagrams helps predict the structure (and hence properties) of alloys, critical for engineering applications.

Key Terminology

The use of greek letters on a phase diagram always represents solid phases.

  • Component: A pure element (e.g. Fe or C) or compound (e.g. water or sugar) that compose the two ends of the phase diagram.
  • Phase: A homogeneous portion of a material with uniform physical and chemical characteristics (e.g., liquid solutions, solid solutions).
  • Solubility Limit: Defines the maximum concentration of solute atoms that can dissolve in a solvent and form a single-phase solution.
  • Solidus Line: Boundary below which the alloy is completely solid.
  • Liquidus Line: Boundary above which the alloy is completely liquid.
  • Solvus Line: Boundary between single-phase solid and two-phase solid regions.
  • Eutectic Point: The lowest temperature at which a mixture of specified components melts completely.
  • Isomorphous System: Binary alloy system with complete solubility in both liquid and solid phases.
  • Homogeneous Materials consist of a single-phase solid solution. For example, small amounts of carbon (< 0.022 wt%) dissolved in iron form a homogeneous solution named ferrite.
  • Heterogeneous Materials consist of mixtures of two or more phases. When the solubility limit is exceeded, an additional phase forms. For example, adding more than 0.022 wt% carbon in iron introduces an additional phase named cementite.
  • Ferrite: Phase in Fe-C alloys (steel) where the carbon composition is < 0.022 wt%. Ferrite has a BCC crystal structure with carbon located in interstitial sites.
  • Cementite: Very hard phase in Fe-C alloys when the carbon composition exceeds 0.022 wt% but below 6.7 wt%. Cementite is also referred to as iron-carbide and has the chemical formula, \(Fe_3C\), with a carbon concentration of 6.7 wt%.
