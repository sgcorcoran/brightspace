Worked Example: Vacancy Concentration

Calculate the equilibrium vacancy concentration for Copper at 25 °C and 800 °C.

Objectives:

  1. Compare the vacancy concentration at elevated and at room temperature.
  2. Apply the Arrhenius equation to calculate the equilibrium vacancy concentration.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the equilibrium vacancy concentration for:

  • Copper at \( T = 800 \, ^\circ\text{C} \text{ and } 25 \,^\circ\text{C}\), with \( Q_v = 0.9 \, \text{eV} \).
  • How many orders of magnitude change in the concentration did you find.

Solution

Copper at 25 °C

The vacancy concentration is given by: \[ N_v = N \exp\left(-\frac{Q_v}{kT}\right) \]

Where:

  • The activation energy for vacancy formation, \( Q_v = 0.9 \, \text{eV} \)
  • The number of possible vacancy sites (lattice sites), \( N = \frac{n}{a^3} \, \text{atoms}/m^3 \)
  • \(n\) is the number of atoms per unit cell
  • \(a\) is the unit cell edge length = \(\frac{4R}{\sqrt{2}}\) for fcc
  • \(R\) is the radius of a copper atom = \(0.140 \text{ nm}\) or \(0.140 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}\)
  • \( T = 298 \, \text{K} \)
  • \( k = 8.617 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{eV/K} \)

Substituting the values:

\[ N = \frac{n}{a^3} = \frac{4}{\left(\frac{4R}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^3} = 6.442\times10^{28} \text{ sites}/m^3\] \[ N_v = 6.442\times10^{28} \exp\left(-\frac{0.9}{8.617 \times 10^{-5} \times 298}\right) = 3.87 \times 10^{13} \text{ vacancies}/m^3\] \[\frac{N_v}{N} = 6 \times 10^{-16}\]

Copper at 800 °C

  • \(T = 1073 \text{ K}\)

Substituting the values:

\[ N_v = 6.442\times10^{28} \exp\left(-\frac{0.9}{8.617 \times 10^{-5} \times 1073}\right) = 3.82 \times 10^{24} \text{ vacancies}/m^3\] \[\frac{N_v}{N} = 6 \times 10^{-5}\]
