Course Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students should be able to:

Broad Course Objectives

  1. Identify and explain the fundamental classifications of engineering materials and their general properties.
  2. Understand the atomic-level structures and bonding mechanisms that dictate materials’ properties.
  3. Apply basic principles of material selection for simple engineering applications.

Chapter 2: Atomic Bonding & Properties

  1. Differentiate between primary (ionic, covalent, metallic) and secondary (hydrogen, van der Waals) bonds and explain their relevance in engineering materials.
  2. Explain how atomic bonding affects physical and mechanical properties like melting point, hardness, electrical conductivity, and ductility.
  3. Interpret bonding energy curves and explain how equilibrium bond length and bonding energy relate to material properties such as stiffness and thermal expansion.
  4. Identify common materials (e.g., metals, ceramics, polymers) and describe the dominant types of bonding present in each.

Chapter 3: Crystalline Structures & Defects

  1. Explain the difference between atomic structure, atomic arrangement, microstructure, and macrostructure.
  2. Identify the 14 Bravais lattices and 7 crystal systems.
  3. Perform crystal structure calculations.
  4. Determine unit cell properties of the FCC and BCC systems.
