Worked Example: Fick’s 1st Law (Given Diffusivity)

Calculate the diffusion flux for a metallic alloy (Iron) and a polymeric material (Polystyrene).

Objectives:

  1. Apply Fick’s 1st Law to determine diffusion flux.
  2. Understand differences in diffusivity between metals and polymers.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the diffusion flux for:

  • Carbon in Iron with \( D = 1 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \), and a concentration gradient of \( \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} = 0.05 \, \text{mol/m}^3/\text{m} \).
  • Oxygen in Polystyrene with \( D = 3 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \), and a concentration gradient of \( \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} = 0.02 \, \text{mol/m}^3/\text{m} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Carbon in Iron

Fick’s 1st Law is given by: \[ J = -D \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} \]

Substitute \( D = 1 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \) and \( \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} = 0.05 \, \text{mol/m}^3/\text{m} \):

\[ J = -(1 \times 10^{-11})(0.05) = -5 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{mol/(m}^2\text{s)} \]

Polymeric Material: Oxygen in Polystyrene

Using \( D = 3 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \) and \( \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} = 0.02 \, \text{mol/m}^3/\text{m} \):

\[ J = -(3 \times 10^{-13})(0.02) = -6 \times 10^{-15} \, \text{mol/(m}^2\text{s)} \]

How does the diffusivity affect the flux for each material?

Discuss why the flux is lower in the polymer compared to the metal.
