Learning Objectives

Chapter 2: Atomic Structure & Bonding

By the end of this chapter, students should be able to:

  1. Differentiate between primary (ionic, covalent, metallic) and secondary (hydrogen, van der Waals) bonds and explain their relevance in engineering materials.
  2. Explain how atomic bonding affects physical and mechanical properties like melting point, hardness, electrical conductivity, and ductility.
  3. Interpret bonding energy curves and explain how equilibrium bond length and bonding energy relate to material properties such as stiffness and thermal expansion.
  4. Identify common materials (e.g., metals, ceramics, polymers) and describe the dominant types of bonding present in each.
