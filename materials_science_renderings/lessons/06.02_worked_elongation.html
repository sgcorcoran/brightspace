Worked Example: Elongation Calculation

Determine the elongation of a metallic (Steel) and a polymeric (Polyethylene) specimen under applied load.

Objectives:

  1. Apply Hooke’s Law to calculate elongation.
  2. Compare elongation behavior between metals and polymers.

Given Problem Statement

Calculate the elongation for:

  • Steel rod: \( L = 2 \, \text{m} \), \( A = 5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \), \( F = 10 \, \text{kN} \), \( E = 200 \, \text{GPa} \).
  • Polyethylene rod: \( L = 1 \, \text{m} \), \( A = 1 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \), \( F = 500 \, \text{N} \), \( E = 1 \, \text{GPa} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Steel

Elongation \( \Delta L \) is given by: \[ \Delta L = \frac{F L}{A E} \]

Substitute values: \[ \Delta L = \frac{(10 \times 10^3)(2)}{(5 \times 10^{-4})(200 \times 10^9)} = 0.2 \, \text{mm} \]

Polymeric Material: Polyethylene

Substitute values for Polyethylene: \[ \Delta L = \frac{(500)(1)}{(1 \times 10^{-4})(1 \times 10^9)} = 5 \, \text{mm} \]
