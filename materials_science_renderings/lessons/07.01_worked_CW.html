Worked Example: Percent Cold Work (%CW)

Calculate the percent cold work for a metallic alloy after deformation and discuss implications for mechanical properties.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the concept of cold work and its effect on materials.
  2. Calculate percent cold work for a deformed metallic alloy.

Key Concept: Percent Cold Work

Cold work refers to the plastic deformation of a material at a temperature below its recrystallization point. It increases dislocation density, leading to strain hardening. The percent cold work is calculated using the change in cross-sectional area:

\[ \%CW = \frac{A_0 - A_f}{A_0} \times 100 \]

Where \( A_0 \) is the initial area and \( A_f \) is the final area.

Expanded Problem Statement

A cylindrical rod of brass with an initial diameter of \( 12 \, \text{mm} \) is reduced to a diameter of \( 10 \, \text{mm} \) through cold working. Calculate the percent cold work and discuss the impact on the material’s yield strength.

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Initial and Final Cross-Sectional Areas

The area of a cylinder is given by \( A = \pi \left(\frac{D}{2}\right)^2 \). Thus:

  • Initial area: \[ A_0 = \pi \left(\frac{12 \, \text{mm}}{2}\right)^2 = 113.1 \, \text{mm}^2 \]
  • Final area: \[ A_f = \pi \left(\frac{10 \, \text{mm}}{2}\right)^2 = 78.5 \, \text{mm}^2 \]

Step 2: Calculate Percent Cold Work

Substitute \( A_0 \) and \( A_f \) into the formula:

\[ \%CW = \frac{113.1 - 78.5}{113.1} \times 100 = 30.6\% \]
