Worked Example: Fick’s 2nd Law (Concentration Profile)

Determine the concentration profile for diffusion in a metallic alloy and a polymer using Fick’s 2nd Law.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the application of Fick’s 2nd Law for non-steady-state diffusion.
  2. Calculate concentration as a function of position and time.

Key Concept: Fick’s 2nd Law

Fick’s 2nd Law describes non-steady-state diffusion, where the concentration changes with both time and position. The general form is:

\[ \frac{\partial C}{\partial t} = D \frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial x^2} \]

The solution for a semi-infinite solid with an initial concentration \( C_0 \) and surface concentration \( C_s \) is:

\[ C(x,t) = C_s + (C_0 - C_s) \text{erf}\left(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{Dt}}\right) \]

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine the concentration profile for:

  • Carbon in Iron at \( t = 2 \, \text{h} \), with \( D = 1 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \), \( C_0 = 0.2 \, \text{wt\%} \), and \( C_s = 1.0 \, \text{wt\%} \).
  • Gas diffusion in Polyethylene at \( t = 1 \, \text{h} \), with \( D = 5 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \), \( C_0 = 0.1 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \), and \( C_s = 0.5 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Carbon in Iron

Calculate the concentration at \( x = 0.5 \, \text{mm} \):

\[ C(0.5\,\text{mm}, 2\,\text{h}) = 1.0 + (0.2 - 1.0)\text{erf}\left(\frac{0.0005}{2\sqrt{(1 \times 10^{-10})(7200)}}\right) \]

Approximating the error function value:

\[ C \approx 0.65 \, \text{wt\%} \]

Polymeric Material: Gas in Polyethylene

For \( x = 1 \, \text{mm} \):

\[ C(1\,\text{mm}, 1\,\text{h}) = 0.5 + (0.1 - 0.5)\text{erf}\left(\frac{0.001}{2\sqrt{(5 \times 10^{-12})(3600)}}\right) \] \[ C \approx 0.3 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \]

Discuss the impact of time on the concentration profile.
