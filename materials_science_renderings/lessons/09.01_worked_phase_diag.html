Worked Example: Phase Diagram Interpretation

Analyze a binary alloy phase diagram and determine phase compositions using the lever rule.

Objectives:

  1. Interpret a binary phase diagram to determine phase compositions and amounts.
  2. Apply the lever rule to solve for mass fractions of different phases.

Key Concept: Lever Rule and Phase Diagram Analysis

The lever rule is used to calculate the mass fractions of phases in a two-phase region of a binary alloy phase diagram. The rule is based on a mass balance and is expressed as:

\[ \text{Fraction of phase 1} = \frac{C_2 - C_0}{C_2 - C_1}, \quad \text{Fraction of phase 2} = \frac{C_0 - C_1}{C_2 - C_1} \]

Where \( C_0 \) is the overall alloy composition, \( C_1 \) is the composition of phase 1, and \( C_2 \) is the composition of phase 2.

Expanded Problem Statement

Consider a binary alloy of \( 60 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \) and \( 40 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{A} \). At \( 600 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), the alloy lies within a two-phase region where the liquid phase (\( L \)) has a composition of \( 70 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \) and the solid phase (\( \alpha \)) has a composition of \( 50 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \). Determine the mass fractions of the liquid and solid phases at this temperature.

Solution

Step 1: Set Up the Lever Rule Equations

Identify the compositions:

  • Overall alloy composition (\( C_0 \)): \( 60 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \)
  • Composition of the solid phase (\( C_1 \)): \( 50 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \)
  • Composition of the liquid phase (\( C_2 \)): \( 70 \, \text{wt\%} \, \text{B} \)

Step 2: Apply the Lever Rule

Calculate the fraction of the solid phase (\( \alpha \)):

\[ \text{Fraction of } \alpha = \frac{C_2 - C_0}{C_2 - C_1} = \frac{70 - 60}{70 - 50} = \frac{10}{20} = 0.5 \]

Calculate the fraction of the liquid phase (\( L \)):

\[ \text{Fraction of } L = \frac{C_0 - C_1}{C_2 - C_1} = \frac{60 - 50}{70 - 50} = \frac{10}{20} = 0.5 \]

Step 3: Verify the Mass Balance

The sum of the phase fractions should equal 1:

\[ 0.5 + 0.5 = 1.0 \]

What changes if the temperature increases to \( 650 \, ^\circ\text{C} \)?

Discuss how the phase compositions and fractions might change with temperature.
