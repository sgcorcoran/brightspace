What is Materials Science and Engineering (MSE)?

Defining MSE

Materials Science and Engineering (MSE) is the interdisciplinary field focused on understanding and applying the properties of materials. The central principle of MSE is that structure affects properties in knowable ways, and processing controls structure, thus allowing control over properties.

MSE combines principles from physics, chemistry, and engineering to design and improve materials for advanced applications. By studying how atoms arrange themselves (structure), how these arrangements influence properties, and how to manipulate them through processing, MSE enables innovation in almost every industry.

Why is MSE Important?

MSE plays a critical role in modern society by enabling technological advancements in:

  • Electronics: Developing semiconductors, superconductors, and batteries by tailoring atomic structure for specific electronic properties.
  • Healthcare: Designing biomedical implants, prosthetics, and drug delivery systems through biocompatible materials with controlled surface properties.
  • Energy: Advancing solar cells, fuel cells, and energy storage by optimizing microstructures for efficiency.
  • Transportation: Creating lightweight composites for automotive and aerospace industries by engineering specific strength-to-weight ratios.

Why is MSE Relevant to You?

Understanding MSE helps explain how and why materials work. Whether you are an engineer, scientist, or simply curious, knowing how materials are designed and controlled helps you appreciate the science behind technologies like smartphones, medical implants, and sustainable packaging.
