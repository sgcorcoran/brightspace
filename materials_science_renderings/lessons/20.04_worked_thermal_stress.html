Worked Example: Thermal Stresses in Composite Materials

Analyze the thermal stresses developed in a composite material consisting of a metallic matrix and a polymeric reinforcement.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the development of thermal stresses in composite materials due to differential thermal expansion.
  2. Calculate the thermal stress in both the matrix and the reinforcement phases.

Key Concept: Thermal Stresses in Composites

Thermal stresses arise in composite materials when there is a mismatch in thermal expansion coefficients between the matrix and reinforcement phases. The thermal stress (\( \sigma_{\text{thermal}} \)) can be calculated using:

\[ \sigma_{\text{thermal}} = E \alpha \Delta T \]

Where \( E \) is the elastic modulus, \( \alpha \) is the coefficient of thermal expansion, and \( \Delta T \) is the change in temperature.

Expanded Problem Statement

Consider a composite material consisting of an aluminum matrix (\( E_{\text{Al}} = 70 \, \text{GPa} \), \( \alpha_{\text{Al}} = 23 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1} \)) and a glass fiber reinforcement (\( E_{\text{GF}} = 90 \, \text{GPa} \), \( \alpha_{\text{GF}} = 5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1} \)). The composite is cooled from \( 200 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) to room temperature (\( 25 \, ^\circ\text{C} \)). Determine the thermal stresses in both the matrix and the reinforcement phases.

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the Change in Temperature

\[ \Delta T = 25 \, ^\circ\text{C} - 200 \, ^\circ\text{C} = -175 \, \text{K} \]

Step 2: Calculate Thermal Stress in the Aluminum Matrix

\[ \sigma_{\text{Al}} = E_{\text{Al}} \alpha_{\text{Al}} \Delta T = (70 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa})(23 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1})(-175 \, \text{K}) \] \[ \sigma_{\text{Al}} = -281.75 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 3: Calculate Thermal Stress in the Glass Fiber Reinforcement

\[ \sigma_{\text{GF}} = E_{\text{GF}} \alpha_{\text{GF}} \Delta T = (90 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa})(5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{K}^{-1})(-175 \, \text{K}) \] \[ \sigma_{\text{GF}} = -78.75 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 4: Interpretation of Results

The aluminum matrix experiences a higher thermal stress due to its larger coefficient of thermal expansion. The mismatch in expansion between the matrix and reinforcement leads to internal stresses that could affect the composite’s integrity.

How could the thermal stresses be minimized in a composite material?

Consider the selection of materials with similar thermal expansion coefficients to reduce stress mismatch.
