Worked Example: Fick’s 1st Law (Arrhenius Dependence)

Calculate the diffusion coefficient using the Arrhenius equation for a metallic alloy (Nickel) and a polymer (Polypropylene).

Objectives:

  1. Determine diffusivity using the Arrhenius equation.
  2. Apply Fick’s 1st Law with calculated diffusivity.

Given Problem Statement

Calculate the diffusion flux for:

  • Nickel at \( T = 1000 \, \text{K} \), with \( D_0 = 2 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \) and \( Q = 250 \, \text{kJ/mol} \).
  • Polypropylene at \( T = 400 \, \text{K} \), with \( D_0 = 1 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \) and \( Q = 80 \, \text{kJ/mol} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Nickel

The Arrhenius equation is: \[ D = D_0 \exp\left(-\frac{Q}{RT}\right) \]

Where \( R = 8.314 \, \text{J/(mol K)} \). Substitute \( D_0 = 2 \times 10^{-5} \), \( Q = 250 \times 10^3 \, \text{J/mol} \), and \( T = 1000 \, \text{K} \):

\[ D = (2 \times 10^{-5}) \exp\left(-\frac{250 \times 10^3}{8.314 \times 1000}\right) \approx 3.9 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \]

Polymeric Material: Polypropylene

Using \( D_0 = 1 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \), \( Q = 80 \times 10^3 \, \text{J/mol} \), and \( T = 400 \, \text{K} \):

\[ D = (1 \times 10^{-7}) \exp\left(-\frac{80 \times 10^3}{8.314 \times 400}\right) \approx 2.8 \times 10^{-13} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \]
