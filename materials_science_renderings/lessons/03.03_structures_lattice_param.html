Lattice Parameters

Triclinic

A set of lattice parameters are used to quantify the crystal structure of a given material.  These lattice parameters, three edge lengths: \( a, b, c \) and 3 angles: \( \alpha, \beta, \gamma \), are shown in the figure for the most general case of the triclinic system where \( a \ne b \ne c \) and \( \alpha \ne \beta \ne \gamma \ne 90^\circ \). 

Cubic

For the cubic system, the edge lengths are all equal (\( a=b=c \)). Also, the angles are all 90 degrees (\( \alpha = \beta = \gamma = 90^\circ \)). We would write this as \((a, a, a, 90, 90, 90)\).

Hexagonal

For the hexagonal system, we would have (a, a, c, 90, 90, 120) where edge lengths a and b are equal but not equal to c and the angles are given as shown in the figure 90°,90°, and 120°. Two additional neighboring unit cells are also shown so that the hexagonal symmetry is evident.
