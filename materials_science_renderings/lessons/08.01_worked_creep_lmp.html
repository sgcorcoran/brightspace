Worked Example: Creep Analysis Using the Larson-Miller Parameter

Determine the rupture time of A286 alloy at an elevated temperature using the Larson-Miller parameter (LMP).

Objectives:

  1. Understand the concept of the Larson-Miller parameter.
  2. Apply given polynomial fit constants for the LMP equation for A286 alloy.
  3. Calculate the rupture time at a specified temperature and stress.

Introduction to the Larson-Miller Parameter (LMP)

Terminology: Larson-Miller Parameter

The Larson-Miller parameter is used to predict the rupture time of materials undergoing creep at high temperatures. It is defined as: \[ P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \] where \(P\) is the Larson-Miller parameter, \(T\) is the temperature in Kelvin, \(C\) is a material-specific constant, and \(t_r\) is the rupture time in hours.

Given Problem Statement

You are given the following cubic polynomial fit for the Larson-Miller parameter as a function of \( \log_{10}(\sigma) \) (where \( \sigma \) is the applied stress in MPa):

\[ P(\sigma) = a_0 + a_1 \log_{10}(\sigma) + a_2 (\log_{10}(\sigma))^2 + a_3 (\log_{10}(\sigma))^3 \]

The coefficients for A286 alloy are:

  • \( a_0 = 116400 \)
  • \( a_1 = -120500 \)
  • \( a_2 = 53460 \)
  • \( a_3 = -8188 \)

Determine the rupture time \(t_r\) at a temperature of \(T = 800 \, ^{\circ} \text{C} \) (or \(1073\, \text{K} \)), given an applied stress of \( \sigma = 140 \, \text{MPa} \). Assume the material constant \( C = 20 \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the Larson-Miller Parameter

Calculate \( \log_{10}(\sigma) \) for the applied stress \( \sigma = 140 \, \text{MPa} \):

\[ \log_{10}(\sigma) = \log_{10}(140) \approx 2.146 \]

Substitute into the polynomial equation:

\[ P = 116400 + (-120500)(2.146) + 53460(2.146)^2 + (-8188)(2.146)^3 \]

After simplifying, we get:

\[ P \approx 23084.59 \]

Step 2: Use the Larson-Miller Parameter Equation

The Larson-Miller parameter equation is: \[ P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \] Rearranging for \( \log_{10} t_r \): \[ \log_{10} t_r = \frac{P}{T} - C \] Substitute \( P = 23084.59 \), \( T = 1073 \, \text{K} \), and \( C = 20 \): \[ \log_{10} t_r = \frac{23084.59}{1073} - 20 \approx 1.514 \]

Step 3: Find the Rupture Time

Using the value of \( \log_{10} t_r \): \[ t_r = 10^{1.514} \approx 32.66 \, \text{hours} \]
