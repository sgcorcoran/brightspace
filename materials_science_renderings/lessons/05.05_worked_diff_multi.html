Worked Example: Diffusion in a Multilayer System

Analyze diffusion through a multilayer structure consisting of a metallic layer and a polymeric layer.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the concept of diffusion across multiple layers with different diffusivities.
  2. Calculate the concentration profile through both metallic and polymeric layers.

Key Concept: Multilayer Diffusion

In a multilayer system, the overall diffusion behavior depends on the properties of each layer. The flux through each layer must be continuous, and the concentration gradient may vary based on the diffusivities. For steady-state diffusion, the flux (\( J \)) is given by:

\[ J = -D \frac{\Delta C}{\Delta x} \]

Where \( D \) is the diffusivity, \( \Delta C \) is the concentration difference, and \( \Delta x \) is the thickness of the layer.

Expanded Problem Statement

Consider a system consisting of a \( 1 \, \text{mm} \) thick copper layer (\( D_{\text{Cu}} = 1 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \)) and a \( 0.5 \, \text{mm} \) thick polyethylene layer (\( D_{\text{PE}} = 5 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \)). The concentration at the surface of the copper layer is \( C_1 = 0.8 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \) and at the surface of the polyethylene layer is \( C_3 = 0.1 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \). Determine the concentration at the interface (\( C_2 \)) and the overall flux through the system.

Solution

Step 1: Define the Flux for Each Layer

For the copper layer:

\[ J_{\text{Cu}} = -D_{\text{Cu}} \frac{C_1 - C_2}{\Delta x_{\text{Cu}}} \]

For the polyethylene layer:

\[ J_{\text{PE}} = -D_{\text{PE}} \frac{C_2 - C_3}{\Delta x_{\text{PE}}} \]

Step 2: Set the Fluxes Equal (Steady-State Condition)

\[ -D_{\text{Cu}} \frac{C_1 - C_2}{\Delta x_{\text{Cu}}} = -D_{\text{PE}} \frac{C_2 - C_3}{\Delta x_{\text{PE}}} \]

Step 3: Solve for \( C_2 \)

Rearrange the equation to solve for the interfacial concentration \( C_2 \):

\[ C_2 = \frac{D_{\text{Cu}} (C_1 \Delta x_{\text{PE}}) + D_{\text{PE}} (C_3 \Delta x_{\text{Cu}})}{D_{\text{Cu}} \Delta x_{\text{PE}} + D_{\text{PE}} \Delta x_{\text{Cu}}} \]

Substitute the given values:

\[ C_2 = \frac{(1 \times 10^{-10})(0.8)(0.5 \times 10^{-3}) + (5 \times 10^{-12})(0.1)(1 \times 10^{-3})}{(1 \times 10^{-10})(0.5 \times 10^{-3}) + (5 \times 10^{-12})(1 \times 10^{-3})} \] \[ C_2 \approx 0.78 \, \text{mol/m}^3 \]

Step 4: Calculate the Overall Flux

Using the copper layer equation for the flux:

\[ J = -D_{\text{Cu}} \frac{C_1 - C_2}{\Delta x_{\text{Cu}}} = -(1 \times 10^{-10}) \frac{0.8 - 0.78}{1 \times 10^{-3}} \] \[ J \approx 2 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{mol/(m}^2\text{s)} \]

Step 5: Interpretation of Results

The concentration at the interface is close to the surface concentration of the copper layer, indicating a higher diffusivity in copper compared to polyethylene. The overall flux is limited by the lower diffusivity of the polyethylene layer.

Discuss the impact of increasing the thickness of the polyethylene layer.

How would this change the concentration profile and the overall flux?
