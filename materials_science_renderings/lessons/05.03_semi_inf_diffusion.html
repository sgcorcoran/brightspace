Worked Example: Semi-Infinite Diffusion

Analyze diffusion behavior in a metallic alloy (Carbon in Iron) and a polymer (Gas in Polyethylene).

Objectives:

  1. Apply the error function solution for semi-infinite diffusion problems.
  2. Understand the different diffusion mechanisms in metals and polymers.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the concentration at \( x = 1 \, \text{mm} \) after \( t = 10 \, \text{h} \) for:

  • Carbon diffusing in Iron at \( D = 1 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \).
  • Oxygen diffusing in Polyethylene at \( D = 5 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{m}^2/\text{s} \).

Solution

Use the error function solution: \[ C(x,t) = C_s \text{erfc}\left(\frac{x}{2\sqrt{Dt}}\right) \]

Metallic Alloy: Carbon in Iron

\[ C(1\,\text{mm}, 10\,\text{h}) \approx C_s \text{erfc}(0.447) \]

Polymeric Material: Oxygen in Polyethylene

\[ C(1\,\text{mm}, 10\,\text{h}) \approx C_s \text{erfc}(0.632) \]
