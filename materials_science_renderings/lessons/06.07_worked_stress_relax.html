Worked Example: Stress Relaxation in Polymers

Analyze the stress relaxation behavior of a polymeric material using the Maxwell model for viscoelasticity.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the concept of stress relaxation in polymers.
  2. Apply the Maxwell model to calculate the decrease in stress over time.

Key Concept: Viscoelastic Behavior and Stress Relaxation

Stress relaxation in polymers occurs when a material under constant strain experiences a decrease in stress over time. The Maxwell model describes this behavior using a combination of a spring (elastic element) and a dashpot (viscous element). The stress relaxation equation is:

\[ \sigma(t) = \sigma_0 \exp\left(-\frac{t}{\tau}\right) \]

Where \( \sigma(t) \) is the stress at time \( t \), \( \sigma_0 \) is the initial stress, and \( \tau \) is the relaxation time, defined as \( \tau = \frac{\eta}{E} \), with \( \eta \) being the viscosity and \( E \) the elastic modulus.

Expanded Problem Statement

A sample of Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) is subjected to a constant strain at \( t = 0 \), resulting in an initial stress of \( \sigma_0 = 5 \, \text{MPa} \). Given that the elastic modulus \( E = 1.5 \, \text{GPa} \) and the viscosity \( \eta = 5 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s} \), determine the stress in the sample after \( 10 \, \text{min} \) and \( 1 \, \text{h} \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the Relaxation Time

The relaxation time \( \tau \) is given by:

\[ \tau = \frac{\eta}{E} = \frac{5 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa} \cdot \text{s}}{1.5 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa}} = 333.3 \, \text{s} \]

Step 2: Calculate Stress at \( t = 10 \, \text{min} \)

Convert time to seconds: \( t = 10 \times 60 = 600 \, \text{s} \).

Substitute into the stress relaxation equation:

\[ \sigma(600) = 5 \, \text{MPa} \times \exp\left(-\frac{600}{333.3}\right) \] \[ \sigma(600) \approx 1.84 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 3: Calculate Stress at \( t = 1 \, \text{h} \)

Convert time to seconds: \( t = 3600 \, \text{s} \).

Substitute into the stress relaxation equation:

\[ \sigma(3600) = 5 \, \text{MPa} \times \exp\left(-\frac{3600}{333.3}\right) \] \[ \sigma(3600) \approx 0.024 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 4: Interpret the Results

The significant decrease in stress over time indicates the viscoelastic nature of PVC, which allows the material to relax stress under constant strain.
