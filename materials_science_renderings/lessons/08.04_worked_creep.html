Worked Example: Creep Strain Rate Calculation

Determine the steady-state creep strain rate for a metallic alloy and a polymer.

Objectives:

  1. Apply the creep strain rate equation to different materials.
  2. Compare the behavior of metals and polymers under creep conditions.

Key Concept: Creep Strain Rate

The steady-state creep rate (\( \dot{\epsilon} \)) is often modeled by the equation:

\[ \dot{\epsilon} = A \sigma^n \exp\left(-\frac{Q}{RT}\right) \]

Where \( A \) is a material constant, \( \sigma \) is the applied stress, \( n \) is the stress exponent, \( Q \) is the activation energy, \( R \) is the gas constant, and \( T \) is the temperature in Kelvin.

Expanded Problem Statement

Calculate the steady-state creep rate for:

  • A286 alloy at \( T = 800 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), with \( \sigma = 140 \, \text{MPa} \), \( Q = 200 \, \text{kJ/mol} \), \( n = 3 \), and \( A = 1 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{s}^{-1} \text{MPa}^{-n} \).
  • Polycarbonate at \( T = 150 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), with \( \sigma = 10 \, \text{MPa} \), \( Q = 120 \, \text{kJ/mol} \), \( n = 2 \), and \( A = 5 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{s}^{-1} \text{MPa}^{-n} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: A286 Alloy

Substitute the given values:

\[ \dot{\epsilon} = (1 \times 10^{-5})(140)^3 \exp\left(-\frac{200 \times 10^3}{8.314 \times 1073}\right) \]

After calculating, we get:

\[ \dot{\epsilon} \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-7} \, \text{s}^{-1} \]

Polymeric Material: Polycarbonate

Substitute the values for Polycarbonate:

\[ \dot{\epsilon} = (5 \times 10^{-6})(10)^2 \exp\left(-\frac{120 \times 10^3}{8.314 \times 423}\right) \] \[ \dot{\epsilon} \approx 2.5 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{s}^{-1} \]

Compare the creep rates of the two materials.

Discuss why the metal exhibits a higher creep rate despite higher activation energy.
