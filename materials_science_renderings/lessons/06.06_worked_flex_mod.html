Worked Example: Young’s Modulus and Flexural Modulus Comparison

Compare the Young’s Modulus and Flexural Modulus of a metallic alloy and a polymeric material.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the relationship between Young’s Modulus and Flexural Modulus.
  2. Calculate and compare these moduli for different materials.

Key Concept: Young’s Modulus vs. Flexural Modulus

Young’s Modulus (\( E \)) measures the stiffness of a material in tension or compression. Flexural Modulus (\( E_f \)) is an equivalent measure when the material is subjected to bending. For isotropic materials, these values are typically similar, but differences can arise due to factors like anisotropy or composite structure.

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine and compare the Young’s Modulus and Flexural Modulus for:

  • A steel sample with \( E = 200 \, \text{GPa} \) and measured flexural modulus of \( 190 \, \text{GPa} \).
  • A polypropylene sample with \( E = 1.5 \, \text{GPa} \) and measured flexural modulus of \( 1.2 \, \text{GPa} \).

Solution

Step 1: Analyze the Steel Sample

Given \( E = 200 \, \text{GPa} \) and \( E_f = 190 \, \text{GPa} \):

The flexural modulus is slightly lower, likely due to the sample experiencing shear deformation during the flexural test.

Calculate the percentage difference:

\[ \text{Difference} = \frac{E - E_f}{E} \times 100 = \frac{200 - 190}{200} \times 100 = 5\% \]

Step 2: Analyze the Polypropylene Sample

Given \( E = 1.5 \, \text{GPa} \) and \( E_f = 1.2 \, \text{GPa} \):

The flexural modulus is lower due to the viscoelastic nature of the polymer, which leads to more pronounced bending deformation.

Calculate the percentage difference:

\[ \text{Difference} = \frac{1.5 - 1.2}{1.5} \times 100 = 20\% \]

Step 3: Interpretation of Results

The difference between Young’s Modulus and Flexural Modulus is more significant for polypropylene due to its lower stiffness and viscoelastic properties compared to the steel sample.

How do material properties influence the relationship between \( E \) and \( E_f \)?

Discuss factors like anisotropy and viscoelasticity that might cause discrepancies between these moduli.
