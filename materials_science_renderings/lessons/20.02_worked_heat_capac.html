Worked Example: Heat Capacity and Thermal Conductivity Analysis

Calculate the heat capacity and thermal conductivity for a metallic alloy and a polymer.

Objectives:

  1. Determine the heat capacity of a material using specific heat values.
  2. Analyze thermal conductivity for both metallic and polymeric materials.

Key Concept: Heat Capacity and Thermal Conductivity

Heat capacity is a measure of the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of a material by one degree. It can be expressed as:

\[ C = m c_p \]

Where \( C \) is the heat capacity, \( m \) is the mass, and \( c_p \) is the specific heat at constant pressure.

Thermal conductivity (\( k \)) describes a material's ability to conduct heat. The rate of heat transfer (\( q \)) through a material can be expressed by Fourier’s law:

\[ q = -k A \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x} \]

Where \( q \) is the heat flux, \( k \) is the thermal conductivity, \( A \) is the cross-sectional area, and \( \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x} \) is the temperature gradient.

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine the heat capacity and thermal conductivity for:

  • An aluminum rod (\( m = 0.5 \, \text{kg} \), \( c_p = 900 \, \text{J/(kg K)} \)), and calculate the heat flux for a temperature difference of \( 100 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) across a length of \( 0.1 \, \text{m} \), with \( k = 237 \, \text{W/(m K)} \).
  • A PVC sample (\( m = 0.2 \, \text{kg} \), \( c_p = 1000 \, \text{J/(kg K)} \)), and calculate the heat flux for a temperature difference of \( 50 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) across a length of \( 0.05 \, \text{m} \), with \( k = 0.2 \, \text{W/(m K)} \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Heat Capacity

Metallic Material: Aluminum

Calculate the heat capacity:

\[ C = m c_p = (0.5 \, \text{kg})(900 \, \text{J/(kg K)}) = 450 \, \text{J/K} \]

Polymeric Material: PVC

Calculate the heat capacity:

\[ C = m c_p = (0.2 \, \text{kg})(1000 \, \text{J/(kg K)}) = 200 \, \text{J/K} \]

Step 2: Calculate Heat Flux Using Fourier’s Law

Aluminum

Substitute \( k = 237 \, \text{W/(m K)} \), \( \Delta T = 100 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), \( \Delta x = 0.1 \, \text{m} \), and assume cross-sectional area \( A = 0.01 \, \text{m}^2 \):

\[ q = -k A \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x} = -(237)(0.01)\left(\frac{100}{0.1}\right) = -2370 \, \text{W} \]

PVC

Substitute \( k = 0.2 \, \text{W/(m K)} \), \( \Delta T = 50 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), \( \Delta x = 0.05 \, \text{m} \), and \( A = 0.01 \, \text{m}^2 \):

\[ q = -k A \frac{\Delta T}{\Delta x} = -(0.2)(0.01)\left(\frac{50}{0.05}\right) = -2 \, \text{W} \]
