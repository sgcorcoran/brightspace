Image Samples

This page includes samples of images.

Best Practices

Images can be added to any of your pages to create visual interest and help break up text-heavy courses. This section illustrates the different ways images can be used.

Follow the directions below to insert new images on other pages.

Replace Image

This template comes with some image placeholders. To replace the image:

  1. Select the image and delete it (Note: Do not move your cursor)
  2. Click the Insert Image icon
  3. Select the source of your image
  4. Click the Choose Destination button to save it to the folder you want

Recommendation for banner image size: at least 1200 pixels wide and 400 pixels high.

Insert New Image

Inserting your own image is simple. Once in the Edit HTML mode, you can use Insert Image to add an image. If the image is not the right size, you can edit it using the inline image editor.

Image Alignment

The Brightspace Editor provides Align Left, Align Right and Align Full functions to place an image in particular location.

For example, if you want your image to appear on the left with the text next to it:

  1. Insert your image on the empty line before the paragraph
  2. Crop the image or set its dimension to your desired size
  3. Apply Align Left on the selected image

Copying Images

To copy any of the following images, please select everything between "start copy" and "end copy" for the specific image, then copy and paste into the Brightspace Editor.

To copy images onto a different page please make sure the page is in 'Edit' mode.

Hockey is a popular ice sport that is enjoyed by people of all ages and skill levels around the world. The game is played with two teams, each consisting of six players - one goaltender and five skaters. The objective of the game is to shoot a small, rubber disc called a puck into the opposing team's net, while preventing the other team from doing the same to your own. Hockey is known for its fast-paced action, physicality, and strategic gameplay.

Hockey has a rich history and culture that spans over a century. The sport originated in Canada in the late 19th century and quickly spread to other parts of the world. Today, hockey is played at both amateur and professional levels, with leagues and tournaments taking place throughout the year. Some of the most prestigious competitions include the National Hockey League (NHL) in North America and the International Ice Hockey Federation (IIHF) World Championships, which feature the best players from around the globe. Hockey has also become a beloved pastime for many fans, who follow their favorite teams and players with passion and enthusiasm.
