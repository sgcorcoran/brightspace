Worked Example: Fatigue Analysis

Estimate the fatigue life of a metallic alloy (AISI 1045 steel) and a polymer (Polycarbonate).

Objectives:

  1. Understand fatigue life estimation using S-N curves.
  2. Apply the fatigue strength formula for both metallic and polymeric materials.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the number of cycles to failure for:

  • AISI 1045 steel with an applied stress of \( \sigma = 300 \, \text{MPa} \).
  • Polycarbonate with an applied stress of \( \sigma = 50 \, \text{MPa} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: AISI 1045 Steel

The fatigue strength equation is: \[ N_f = \left(\frac{\sigma_f'}{\sigma}\right)^{1/b} \]

Where:

  • \( \sigma_f' = 950 \, \text{MPa} \) (fatigue strength coefficient)
  • \( b = -0.12 \) (fatigue strength exponent)
  • \( \sigma = 300 \, \text{MPa} \) (applied stress)

Calculate the number of cycles to failure:

\[ N_f = \left(\frac{950}{300}\right)^{1/(-0.12)} \approx 10^5 \, \text{cycles} \]

Polymeric Material: Polycarbonate

Using a simplified approach for polymers, assume \( b = -0.15 \) and \( \sigma_f' = 80 \, \text{MPa} \):

\[ N_f = \left(\frac{80}{50}\right)^{1/(-0.15)} \approx 2 \times 10^4 \, \text{cycles} \]

What would happen if the applied stress is reduced?

Consider how lowering the stress level impacts fatigue life for both materials.
