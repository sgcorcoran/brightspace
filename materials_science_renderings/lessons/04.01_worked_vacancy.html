Worked Example: Vacancy Concentration

Calculate the equilibrium vacancy concentration for Copper and Polyethylene at elevated temperatures.

Objectives:

  1. Understand vacancy formation in metals and polymers.
  2. Apply the Arrhenius equation for vacancy concentration.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the equilibrium vacancy concentration for:

  • Copper at \( T = 900 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), with \( Q_v = 0.9 \, \text{eV} \).
  • Polyethylene at \( T = 150 \, ^\circ\text{C} \), considering free volume effects.

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Copper

The vacancy concentration is given by: \[ n_v = n \exp\left(-\frac{Q_v}{kT}\right) \]

Where:

  • \( Q_v = 0.9 \, \text{eV} \)
  • \( T = 1173 \, \text{K} \)
  • \( k = 8.617 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{eV/K} \)

Substituting the values:

\[ n_v = n \exp\left(-\frac{0.9}{8.617 \times 10^{-5} \times 1173}\right) \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-4} \]

Polymeric Material: Polyethylene

For polymers, vacancy-like defects relate to free volume. Estimate \( n_v \approx 10^{-3} \) based on typical free volume at \( T = 423 \, \text{K} \).
