Atomic Arrangement of Atoms

In this chapter, we will explore the atomic arrangement of atoms in both crystalline and non-crystalline structures. Our primary focus will be crystalline structures in metals and ceramics but we will also touch briefly on polymers. An in-depth look at polymers, will take place in a later chapter. We will see that materials can often take on more than one structure. Iron for example has a body centered cubic structure at room temperature but will transform to face centered cubic at higher temperatures. The meaning of these terms will become clearer as we move through this chapter.
