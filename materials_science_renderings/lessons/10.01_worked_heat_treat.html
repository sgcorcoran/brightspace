Worked Example: Phase Transformation and Heat Treatment

Analyze the phase transformation in a steel alloy and the change in crystallinity of a polymer due to heat treatment.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the impact of heat treatment on phase transformation in metals and crystallinity in polymers.
  2. Calculate phase fractions using TTT diagrams and analyze changes in crystallinity using DSC data.

Key Concept: Phase Transformation and Crystallinity

Phase transformations in metals involve changes in the crystal structure due to temperature variations. The TTT diagram helps predict the phase fractions at different temperatures and cooling rates. For polymers, heat treatment can alter the degree of crystallinity, which affects the material's properties. Crystallinity is often analyzed using Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC), which measures the heat flow associated with phase transitions.

Expanded Problem Statement

Analyze the following heat treatment scenarios:

  • A steel alloy (0.8% Carbon) is cooled rapidly from \( 800 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) to room temperature. Use the TTT diagram to estimate the fraction of martensite formed.
  • A sample of Polyethylene is heat-treated and analyzed using DSC. The melting enthalpy (\( \Delta H_m \)) is measured as \( 180 \, \text{J/g} \), and the theoretical enthalpy of fusion for 100% crystalline Polyethylene is \( 290 \, \text{J/g} \). Determine the degree of crystallinity.

Solution

Step 1: Analyze Phase Transformation in Steel Using the TTT Diagram

For a 0.8% Carbon steel cooled rapidly, the TTT diagram indicates complete martensite formation at room temperature. The fraction of martensite (\( f_m \)) is:

\[ f_m = 1.0 \text{ (100% martensite)} \]

Given the rapid cooling rate, the austenite does not have sufficient time to transform into pearlite or bainite.

Step 2: Calculate the Degree of Crystallinity for Polyethylene

The degree of crystallinity (\( X_c \)) is given by:

\[ X_c = \frac{\Delta H_m}{\Delta H_{m,100\%}} \times 100 \]

Substitute the given values:

\[ X_c = \frac{180 \, \text{J/g}}{290 \, \text{J/g}} \times 100 = 62.1\% \]

Step 3: Interpretation of Results

The steel alloy exhibits a complete martensitic transformation due to rapid cooling, resulting in a hard and brittle microstructure. The Polyethylene sample shows a moderate degree of crystallinity, affecting its mechanical properties, such as stiffness and toughness.

How would slower cooling affect the phase transformation in steel?

Consider the formation of bainite or pearlite at lower cooling rates and the implications for mechanical properties.
