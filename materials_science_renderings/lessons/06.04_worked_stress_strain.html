Worked Example: Young’s Modulus and Stress-Strain Analysis

Analyze the stress-strain behavior of a metallic alloy (Steel) and a polymer (Polycarbonate) using Young’s Modulus.

Objectives:

  1. Apply the concept of Young’s Modulus to determine stress and strain values.
  2. Compare the mechanical behavior of metals and polymers under tensile loading.

Key Concept: Young’s Modulus

Young’s Modulus (\( E \)) is a measure of a material's stiffness and is defined as the ratio of stress to strain in the linear elastic region:

\[ E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon} \]

It is a fundamental property that characterizes the elastic behavior of both metals and polymers.

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine the stress, strain, and elongation for:

  • A steel sample with a cross-sectional area of \( 5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \), subjected to a load of \( 50 \, \text{kN} \). Young’s Modulus for steel is \( 210 \, \text{GPa} \).
  • A polycarbonate sample with a cross-sectional area of \( 2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \), subjected to a load of \( 2 \, \text{kN} \). Young’s Modulus for polycarbonate is \( 2.3 \, \text{GPa} \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Stress

Stress is defined as \( \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \).

Metallic Material: Steel

Substitute \( F = 50 \, \text{kN} \) and \( A = 5 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \):

\[ \sigma = \frac{50 \times 10^3}{5 \times 10^{-4}} = 100 \times 10^6 \, \text{Pa} = 100 \, \text{MPa} \]

Polymeric Material: Polycarbonate

Substitute \( F = 2 \, \text{kN} \) and \( A = 2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \):

\[ \sigma = \frac{2 \times 10^3}{2 \times 10^{-4}} = 10 \times 10^6 \, \text{Pa} = 10 \, \text{MPa} \]

Step 2: Calculate Strain

Strain is given by \( \epsilon = \frac{\sigma}{E} \).

Steel

\[ \epsilon = \frac{100 \times 10^6}{210 \times 10^9} = 4.76 \times 10^{-4} \]

Polycarbonate

\[ \epsilon = \frac{10 \times 10^6}{2.3 \times 10^9} = 4.35 \times 10^{-3} \]

Step 3: Calculate Elongation

Elongation is determined by \( \Delta L = \epsilon \times L_0 \). Assume initial length \( L_0 = 1 \, \text{m} \) for both materials.

Steel

\[ \Delta L = (4.76 \times 10^{-4})(1 \, \text{m}) = 0.476 \, \text{mm} \]

Polycarbonate

\[ \Delta L = (4.35 \times 10^{-3})(1 \, \text{m}) = 4.35 \, \text{mm} \]

Discuss the difference in elongation between steel and polycarbonate.
