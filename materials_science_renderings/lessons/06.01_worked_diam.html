Worked Example: Load to Produce Diameter Change (Elastic Deformation)

Calculate the load required to produce a specified diameter change for a metallic alloy (Aluminum) and a polymer (Nylon).

Objectives:

  1. Understand the relationship between applied load and elastic deformation.
  2. Calculate the load required for diameter change using Hooke’s Law.

Given Problem Statement

Determine the load required to produce a diameter reduction of \(0.5 \, \text{mm}\) for:

  • Aluminum rod with original diameter \(D_0 = 20 \, \text{mm}\), length \(L = 1 \, \text{m}\), and \( E = 70 \, \text{GPa} \).
  • Nylon rod with original diameter \(D_0 = 10 \, \text{mm}\), length \(L = 0.5 \, \text{m}\), and \( E = 2.8 \, \text{GPa} \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Aluminum

Using Hooke’s Law, the relationship between stress, strain, and Young’s modulus is: \[ \sigma = E \epsilon \]

Strain \( \epsilon \) for a diameter change is given by: \[ \epsilon = \frac{\Delta D}{D_0} = \frac{-0.5 \, \text{mm}}{20 \, \text{mm}} = -0.025 \]

Calculate the stress: \[ \sigma = (70 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa})(-0.025) = -1.75 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa} \]

Determine the load \( F \) using \( \sigma = \frac{F}{A} \):

\[ A = \pi \left(\frac{D_0}{2}\right)^2 = \pi (0.01 \, \text{m})^2 = 3.14 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{m}^2 \] \[ F = \sigma \times A = (-1.75 \times 10^9)(3.14 \times 10^{-4}) = -549.5 \, \text{kN} \]

Polymeric Material: Nylon

For Nylon, repeat the same procedure:

Strain: \[ \epsilon = \frac{-0.5 \, \text{mm}}{10 \, \text{mm}} = -0.05 \]

Stress: \[ \sigma = (2.8 \times 10^9 \, \text{Pa})(-0.05) = -1.4 \times 10^8 \, \text{Pa} \]

Load: \[ F = \sigma \times A \] where \( A = \pi (0.005)^2 = 7.85 \times 10^{-5} \, \text{m}^2 \):

\[ F = (-1.4 \times 10^8)(7.85 \times 10^{-5}) = -11.0 \, \text{kN} \]

Discuss the difference in load required for Aluminum vs. Nylon.
