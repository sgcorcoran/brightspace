Worked Example: Creep Rupture Time Prediction

Predict the rupture time of a metallic alloy and a polymeric material using the Larson-Miller parameter.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the concept of creep and its importance in material failure.
  2. Apply the Larson-Miller parameter to predict the time to rupture under specified conditions.

Key Concept: Larson-Miller Parameter and Creep

Creep is the time-dependent deformation of materials under constant stress, typically occurring at high temperatures. The Larson-Miller parameter (LMP) is used to predict rupture time based on temperature and stress:

\[ P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \]

Where \( P \) is the Larson-Miller parameter, \( T \) is the temperature in Kelvin, \( C \) is a material constant, and \( t_r \) is the rupture time in hours.

Expanded Problem Statement

Predict the rupture time for:

  • A286 alloy at \( T = 800 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) and \( \sigma = 150 \, \text{MPa} \). Given the Larson-Miller parameter equation \( P = 116400 - 120500 \log_{10}(\sigma) + 53460 (\log_{10}(\sigma))^2 - 8188 (\log_{10}(\sigma))^3 \) with \( C = 20 \).
  • Polycarbonate at \( T = 150 \, ^\circ\text{C} \) and \( \sigma = 20 \, \text{MPa} \), using \( C = 18 \) and assuming a simplified form \( P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the Larson-Miller Parameter for A286 Alloy

Determine \( \log_{10}(\sigma) \) for \( \sigma = 150 \, \text{MPa} \):

\[ \log_{10}(\sigma) = \log_{10}(150) \approx 2.176 \]

Substitute into the polynomial equation for \( P \):

\[ P = 116400 - 120500(2.176) + 53460(2.176)^2 - 8188(2.176)^3 \] \[ P \approx 23150 \]

Step 2: Calculate the Rupture Time for A286 Alloy

Using the Larson-Miller equation \( P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \), rearrange for \( \log_{10} t_r \):

\[ \log_{10} t_r = \frac{P}{T} - C = \frac{23150}{1073} - 20 \approx 1.56 \] \[ t_r = 10^{1.56} \approx 36 \, \text{hours} \]

Step 3: Calculate the Rupture Time for Polycarbonate

For Polycarbonate at \( T = 423 \, \text{K} \) and \( C = 18 \):

\[ P = T (C + \log_{10} t_r) \implies \log_{10} t_r = \frac{P}{T} - C \]

Assume \( P = 9000 \) based on typical polymer data:

\[ \log_{10} t_r = \frac{9000}{423} - 18 \approx 0.28 \] \[ t_r = 10^{0.28} \approx 1.9 \, \text{hours} \]

Step 4: Interpretation of Results

The metallic alloy has a significantly longer rupture time compared to the polymer, indicating better high-temperature performance under stress.
