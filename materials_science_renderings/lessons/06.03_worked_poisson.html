Worked Example: Poisson’s Ratio and Transverse Strain

Calculate the transverse strain and discuss the significance of Poisson’s ratio for a metallic alloy and a polymer.

Objectives:

  1. Understand the relationship between axial and transverse strain using Poisson’s ratio.
  2. Calculate transverse strain for different materials under uniaxial stress.

Key Concept: Poisson’s Ratio

Poisson’s ratio (\( \nu \)) is a measure of the ratio of transverse strain (\( \epsilon_t \)) to axial strain (\( \epsilon_a \)) in a material under uniaxial stress. It is defined as:

\[ \nu = -\frac{\epsilon_t}{\epsilon_a} \]

Typical values for metals range from 0.25 to 0.35, while polymers often exhibit higher values due to their flexibility.

Expanded Problem Statement

Determine the transverse strain for:

  • A steel rod subjected to a tensile stress of \( 200 \, \text{MPa} \), with \( E = 210 \, \text{GPa} \) and \( \nu = 0.3 \).
  • A polyethylene rod under a tensile stress of \( 20 \, \text{MPa} \), with \( E = 1 \, \text{GPa} \) and \( \nu = 0.45 \).

Solution

Metallic Alloy: Steel

Calculate the axial strain (\( \epsilon_a \)) using Hooke’s Law:

\[ \epsilon_a = \frac{\sigma}{E} = \frac{200 \times 10^6}{210 \times 10^9} = 9.52 \times 10^{-4} \]

Determine the transverse strain (\( \epsilon_t \)):

\[ \epsilon_t = -\nu \cdot \epsilon_a = -0.3 \times 9.52 \times 10^{-4} = -2.86 \times 10^{-4} \]

Polymeric Material: Polyethylene

Calculate the axial strain:

\[ \epsilon_a = \frac{20 \times 10^6}{1 \times 10^9} = 2 \times 10^{-2} \]

Determine the transverse strain:

\[ \epsilon_t = -0.45 \times 2 \times 10^{-2} = -9 \times 10^{-3} \]

Compare the transverse strains for steel and polyethylene.

Discuss why the polymer exhibits a higher transverse strain compared to the metal.
