Worked Example: Electrical Conductivity Calculation

Determine the electrical conductivity of a metallic alloy and a polymeric material using given properties.

Objectives:

  1. Calculate electrical conductivity from resistivity and material properties.
  2. Compare electrical behavior between metals and polymers.

Key Concept: Electrical Conductivity

Electrical conductivity (\( \sigma \)) is a measure of a material's ability to conduct electric current. It is inversely related to resistivity (\( \rho \)), defined as:

\[ \sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} \]

For metals, conductivity is typically high due to the presence of free electrons, while polymers generally exhibit low conductivity because they lack free charge carriers.

Expanded Problem Statement

Calculate the electrical conductivity for:

  • A copper wire with a resistivity of \( 1.7 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m} \).
  • A polyethylene sample with a resistivity of \( 1 \times 10^{14} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m} \).

Solution

Step 1: Calculate Electrical Conductivity

Metallic Material: Copper

Substitute the resistivity value for copper:

\[ \sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} = \frac{1}{1.7 \times 10^{-8} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m}} = 5.88 \times 10^7 \, \text{S/m} \]

Polymeric Material: Polyethylene

Substitute the resistivity value for polyethylene:

\[ \sigma = \frac{1}{\rho} = \frac{1}{1 \times 10^{14} \, \Omega \cdot \text{m}} = 1 \times 10^{-14} \, \text{S/m} \]

Step 2: Analyze the Results

The electrical conductivity of copper is significantly higher than that of polyethylene. This difference highlights the disparity between conductive metals and insulating polymers.
